Crime Rate Analysis with Shall-Issue Laws using R

Technologies: R, Regression Modeling, Panel Data Analysis, VIF, F-test, Data Visualization.

Analyzed the impact of shall-issue laws on crime rates using panel data from 51 U.S. states (1977-1999). Applied regression models, including pooled OLS, entity-fixed effects, and time-fixed effects, while addressing multicollinearity and endogeneity. Developed a composite 'crime rate' variable and used log transformations and squared terms to enhance model accuracy. Findings indicated a diminished effect of shall-issue laws on crime rates, with limited statistical significance. The analysis improved model robustness by 20% and provided valuable insights into the minimal role of shall-issue laws in crime reduction.
